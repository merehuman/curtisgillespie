• CURTIS GILLESPIE

Curtis Gillespie has written five books, including the memoirs Almost There and Playing Through and the novel Crown Shyness, which have won or been nominated for a variety of awards. His journalism and non-fiction have appeared in such outlets at The Paris Review, The Walrus, The New York Times, Western Living, New Trail, Alberta Views, Toro, Saturday Night, enRoute and many others. He has won seven National Magazine Awards, including a record-tying four awards in 2014. In 2010, he co-founded (with Lynn Coady) the narrative journalism magazine Eighteen Bridges, which he edits. In addition to his writing and editing, he has taught and mentored many writers through different teaching roles over the years.

He also volunteers in various capacities and was President of Litfest, Edmonton’s Non-Fiction Literary festival for eight years. He was a co-founder and for ten years President of the Nina Haggerty Centre for the Arts, a visual arts school for persons with developmental disabilities. Currently, he sits on the boards of the National Magazine Awards Foundation and the Canadian Literature Centre. In 2014, he received the University of Alberta’s Alumni Honour Award, in 2015 the Edmonton Artist’s Trust Fund Award and in 2016 was named Editor of the Year at the Alberta Magazine Publishers Association Awards. Eighteen Bridges was named Magazine of the Year at the 2018 AMPA awards.

In addition to his literary activities, he also is an internationally accredited squash official and has refereed at tournaments such as the World Championships and the Tournament of Champions. He lives in Edmonton with his wife, Cathy. They have two daughters who have abandoned their parents (though they call it moving out and starting their own lives).
